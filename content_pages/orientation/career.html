MSHE Career Coaching: Ali Segal 

The MS in Higher Education Administration and Policy Program offers students and alumni of the program both programmatic and individual support for career preparation and advancement. Students are invited to participate in workshops throughout the year that address key topics in career preparation and advancement. Additionally, the MSHE Career Coach, Alison Segal, is available by appointment to assist students and alumni with resumes, cover letters, interview preparation, networking, job search help and general career advising. 

Ali has been working in higher education and the high school-to-college transition space for over ten years. She began her career in the profession in undergraduate admissions at the University of Chicago, where she oversaw international admissions and was a member of the founding working group of the Collegiate Scholars Program. Subsequently she worked as a high school college counselor in both public and private school settings, and most recently, for college access programs in the US and the UK. Ali is a 2008 graduate of the MSHE program. To set an appointment with Ali Segal, email mshe-careers@northwestern.edu.

Learning to (Best) Use LinkedIn

It is easy to get started with the basics of LinkedIn. If you're at all familiar with social media you'll be able to figure out how to set up your profile without much trouble. However, most of LinkedIn's most useful features are a bit hidden. If you're a LinkedIn beginner, below are some short (5 minute) videos about how to use LinkedIn. These videos are targeted to college students who are beginning their job search, so they are highly relevant to MSHE students.
